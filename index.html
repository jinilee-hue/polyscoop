Tech Show and Tell

From humanoid robots to AI-powered gadgets — CES 2026 showed the world what tomorrow looks like, through a student reporter's eyes.

JK
Jason Kim
Songpa Campus
2026.01.08
✨ AI Generated
LG Electronics' humanoid robot, unveiled at CES 2026, Las Vegas. Designed to handle household tasks autonomously. (Illustration: AI-generated by Poly Scoop)

Tech fans attended the Consumer Electronics Show (CES) from January 6 to 9. The yearly showcase takes place in Las Vegas, Nevada. It allows tech companies to show off their newest products.

This year, many companies presented robots. For example, Korean tech giant LG Electronics presented its new humanoid bot. LG announced that the bot is designed to handle "time-consuming" household tasks, such as folding laundry.

But robots were not the only stars of the show. Many other companies brought exciting new gadgets and ideas to the stage. From smart glasses that translate languages in real time, to tiny projectors that fit in your pocket, CES 2026 had something for everyone who loves technology.

For students like us, the most exciting part was seeing how AI is becoming a bigger part of everyday life. Experts at the show explained that AI can help people work faster and learn new things more easily. Some even said that in the future, AI tutors might help students study at home.

Of course, not everyone is happy about robots doing more work. Some people worry that robots might take away jobs from humans. But many tech leaders at CES said that robots and humans can work together as a team — each doing what they do best.

As we look at all these amazing inventions, one thing is clear: the future is arriving faster than we think. And as young students of today, we will be the ones who decide how to use these technologies wisely.
